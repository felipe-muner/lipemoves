# Music Guide — Every Reel Has a Sound
### What to search, where to find it, and why it matches each video

---

## How to Use This Guide

Two ways to add music:
1. **CapCut** — built-in library, royalty-free, safe for all platforms. Search the terms below directly in the app.
2. **Instagram trending audio** — when you find a sound trending in your niche, use it. The algorithm boosts reels that use trending audio. Check what @mateus.yoga or @kestyoga are using this week.

> For talking-head reels: music at 10–15% volume max. It's texture, not the focus.  
> For flow/movement reels: music at 60–80% volume. Let it breathe.

---

### Reel 1 — Morning Mobility Routine
**Mood:** Calm, slow, waking up. Like a Sunday morning before anyone else is awake.

**Search in CapCut:** `"morning lo-fi"` `"calm ambient morning"` `"soft piano lo-fi"`

**Reference artists** (search on Spotify/YouTube to feel the vibe first):
- Idealism — *Serenity* (lo-fi, gentle piano)
- j^p^n — any track (ambient lo-fi, no lyrics)
- Kupla — *Sip* (soft, warm, slow)

**What to avoid:** anything with drops, bass, or fast tempo. This video is about stillness.

**Option:** No music at all — just ambient sound (mat, breath, birds outside). This actually performs really well for morning content. Try both and see which feels right.

### Reel 2 — Kettlebell Halos (Wrong vs Right)
**Mood:** Focused, deliberate. Like a craftsman working slowly and precisely.

**Search in CapCut:** `"minimal beats"` `"dark lo-fi instrumental"` `"focus beats"`

**Reference artists:**
- Tomppabeats — *Harbor* (lo-fi with subtle groove)
- Philanthrope — any track (smooth, slightly rhythmic lo-fi)
- Chillhop Music compilations (search on YouTube — these are all royalty-free)

**What to avoid:** anything too soft or ambient — this reel has two distinct sections (wrong/right) and needs slight energy to carry the contrast.

### Reel 3 — Why I Mix Everything (Talking Head)
**Mood:** Personal, honest, direct. This is a conversation, not a performance.

**Search in CapCut:** `"soft background instrumental"` `"cinematic background"` `"subtle ambient"`

**Reference artists:**
- Nils Frahm — *Says* (piano + texture, beautiful but subtle)
- Olafur Arnalds — *Near Light* (same energy)

**Volume:** 10% maximum. You're talking. Music is just warmth in the background.

**Alternative:** No music. Talking-head content often performs better in silence — it feels more real and urgent. Test it.

## General Rules

### Do
- Match the energy of the movement to the energy of the music
- Use the same artist/style across a week for cohesion
- Save tracks you find in a CapCut playlist so you reuse them — consistency builds audio brand identity
- Check what's trending in the Reels audio section weekly (tap the audio name on any reel you see in your explore)

### Don't
- Use anything with lyrics for movement reels — lyrics fight with your text overlays and voice
- Use the same track two weeks in a row — vary it enough to stay fresh
- Use generic "gym motivation" music — it puts you in the wrong niche (you're not that)

---

## Trending Audio Hack

Once a week (Sunday when you're scheduling):
1. Open Instagram Explore
2. Watch 5 reels from accounts in your niche
3. Tap the audio name on any reel that's performing well
4. If it says "Trending" — save it and consider using it that week

Instagram explicitly boosts reels that use trending audio. This is free reach.
