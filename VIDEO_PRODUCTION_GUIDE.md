# Video Production Guide — What to Record

> This is your filming bible. Each video has a title, hook, what to do on camera, how to frame it, and what to say.

---

## WEEK 1 — "Why I train this way"
### 3 Reels to record

---

### REEL 1 — Monday
**Title:** Morning Mobility Routine  
**Pillar:** Move  
**Duration:** 30–40s  
**Vibe:** Calm, no music — just ambient sound or light lo-fi

**What to film:**
A flowing sequence of 4–5 movements done back to back, slow and intentional. Suggested flow:
1. Cat-cow (5 reps)
2. Child's pose → thread the needle (each side)
3. Hip 90/90 transition (slow, both sides)
4. World's greatest stretch (each side)
5. Downward dog → cobra flow (3 reps)

**Hook (first 2 seconds):**  
Start already moving — mid cat-cow. Text overlay: *"This is how I start every morning."*

**How to frame it:**  
Wide angle, slightly low to the ground, natural light. Show your full body. Keep the camera still on a tripod/stack of books. No jump cuts — continuous flow looks better for this one.

**What to say (voice or text overlay):**  
No voiceover needed. Just ambient sound + text overlays naming each movement as you do it.

**Caption hook:**  
*"5 minutes. No gym. No excuses. This is my morning ritual."*

---

### REEL 2 — Friday
**Title:** Kettlebell Halos — Form & Why It Matters  
**Pillar:** Build  
**Duration:** 40–50s  
**Vibe:** Educational, slow, deliberate

**What to film:**  
Start facing camera. Pick up a light-medium kettlebell.  
- Show the WRONG way first (rushed, shrugged shoulders, no control) — 5s  
- Then show the RIGHT way — slow halos, shoulder packed, ribs down — 20s  
- Then do 5 slow reps with text overlay explaining each cue

**Hook (first 2 seconds):**  
Hold up the kettlebell to camera and say (or text overlay): *"Most people waste this drill. Here's how to actually do it."*

**How to frame it:**  
Camera at chest height, slightly angled. Side angle for some reps so people can see the shoulder position. You can cut between front and side.

**What to say:**
- "Ribs down, don't arch your low back"
- "Control the weight, don't let it pull you"
- "This is shoulder health for life"

**Caption hook:**  
*"If you have tight shoulders — this drill is for you. But only if you do it like this."*

---

### REEL 3 — (Bonus / Buffer for week 1 or early week 2)
**Title:** Why I Mix Yoga + Kettlebell + Calisthenics  
**Pillar:** BTS / Personal  
**Duration:** 30–45s  
**Vibe:** Talking head + B-roll cuts

**What to film:**  
Two parts:
1. **Talking head** (10–15s): Look at camera and say your "why" — something like: *"I used to just lift heavy. I was strong but couldn't touch my toes. Then I started mixing movement practices and everything changed."*
2. **B-roll montage** (20–25s): Quick cuts of — you doing a handstand attempt, a yoga pose, swinging a kettlebell, a deep squat, a back bend. Fast cuts, 2–3s each.

**How to frame it:**  
Talking head: camera at eye level, clean background or outdoor. B-roll: close-ups and wide shots mixed.

**Caption hook:**  
*"I don't train for looks. I train to move well at 80."*

## WEEK 2 — "Teach something every day"
### 3 Reels to record

---

### REEL 4 — Monday
**Title:** Hip 90/90 — The Drill Your Lower Back Needs  
**Pillar:** Longevity  
**Duration:** 45–60s  
**Vibe:** Tutorial, grounded, educational

**What to film:**  
Sit on the floor in 90/90 position. Film yourself doing:
1. Just sitting upright in the position — many people can't do this
2. Slow forward lean over front shin
3. Hip switch side to side (the transition)
4. Internal rotation press (pushing knee down)

**Hook (first 2 seconds):**  
Text overlay + you sitting in position: *"Sit like this for 2 minutes a day. Your back will thank you in 10 years."*

**How to frame it:**  
Slightly elevated angle so the viewer can see your hips clearly. Side angle for the lean. Use slow-motion on the hip switch.

**What to say (voiceover or text):**
- "Most lower back pain comes from stiff hips"
- "Your hip can't move, so your lumbar takes the stress"
- "This drill teaches your hip to rotate — properly"

**Caption hook:**  
*"Lower back pain? Your hips are probably the problem. Here's the fix."*

---

### REEL 5 — Friday
**Title:** Kettlebell Armbar — The Move Nobody Talks About  
**Pillar:** Build  
**Duration:** 45s  
**Vibe:** Calm tutorial, slow and precise

**What to film:**  
Lie on the floor. Show the kettlebell armbar:
1. Start position — KB pressed up, arm vertical
2. Slow rollover, keeping eye on the KB
3. Hip extension at the bottom
4. Return slowly

Do 3–4 reps each side, slow. Film from above (prop phone on something high) and from the side.

**Hook:**  
*"This is the most underrated shoulder drill in existence."*

**What to say:**
- "Active shoulder — don't let it collapse"
- "This builds rotator cuff strength AND thoracic mobility"
- "Yoga + kettlebell in one move"

**Caption hook:**  
*"One move. Shoulder stability. Thoracic mobility. That's the deal."*

---

### REEL 6 — (Midweek / Buffer)
**Title:** 3-Minute Wrist Prep — Do This Before Any Session  
**Pillar:** Move  
**Duration:** 30–40s  
**Vibe:** Quick, practical, save-worthy

**What to film:**  
Kneeling on the floor. Show a wrist warm-up sequence:
1. Wrist circles both directions
2. Fingers forward, lean forward (stretch)
3. Fingers back, lean forward (extension)
4. Palm-down press (knuckle push-up position hold)
5. Prayer hands, elbows out

**Hook:**  
*"Before you do any push-up, handstand, or downward dog — do this first."*

**How to frame it:**  
Close-up of hands is important here. Film from above for circles, side for the stretches.

**Caption hook:**  
*"Wrists always hurt during training? You're skipping this."*

## WEEK 3 — "Invite people in"
### 3 Reels to record

---

### REEL 7 — Monday (Challenge Launch)
**Title:** 7-Day Spine Health Challenge — Day 1  
**Pillar:** Move + Community  
**Duration:** 45–60s  
**Vibe:** Energetic but grounded, clear instructions

**What to film:**  
This launches a 7-day challenge. Day 1 = spinal flexion/extension.  
Show the daily drill for Day 1:
1. Seated cat-cow
2. Standing side bend (each side)
3. Gentle thoracic rotation seated

Film yourself doing a clean set of each. Text overlay counts the reps.

**Hook:**  
Start with text: *"Day 1 of 7 — Spine Mobility Challenge."* Then: *"Do this with me every day this week."*

**What to say:**  
"Follow me and I'll post each day's drill. 5 minutes. Every day. Your spine will feel different by day 7."

**Caption:**  
Include the challenge rules: follow you, do each drill, tag you in their story.

### REEL 8 — Friday (Challenge Day 5)
**Title:** 7-Day Challenge — Day 5: Hip Flexor Release  
**Pillar:** Longevity  
**Duration:** 45s

**What to film:**  
Low lunge position (kneeling). Show:
1. Static hold — 30s each side
2. Gentle oscillations forward/back
3. Adding thoracic rotation in the lunge
4. Optional: back foot elevated on wall

**Hook:**  
*"Day 5. Sitting kills your hips. Let's fix that."*

### REEL 9 — (Midweek)
**Title:** The Cossack Squat — Mobility + Strength in One  
**Pillar:** Build  
**Duration:** 45s  
**Vibe:** Tutorial, show progressions

**What to film:**  
Show 3 levels of the cossack squat:
1. **Beginner:** Hands on floor for support, partial range
2. **Intermediate:** Hands at chest, full range
3. **Advanced:** Slow tempo, add a reach/rotation

Film from the front so people can see the hips and feet clearly.

**Hook:**  
*"One move. Inner thighs. Hips. Ankle mobility. Glute strength. You're welcome."*

**Caption hook:**  
*"If you can't do a full cossack squat yet — start with level 1. I'll show you how."*

## WEEK 4 — "Authority & Soft Sell"
### 3 Reels to record

---

### REEL 10 — Monday (Most Polished)
**Title:** Full Morning Flow — 5 Minutes  
**Pillar:** Move  
**Duration:** 60s  
**Vibe:** This is your best-produced video of the month. Clean, aesthetic, intentional.

**What to film:**  
A complete 5-minute morning flow edited down to 60s. Sequence:
1. Seated breath (2 breaths, shown)
2. Cat-cow x5
3. Child's pose → cobra flow x3
4. Down dog → lunge → twist (each side)
5. 90/90 hip switch x4
6. Standing — ground yourself, arms up

**Production notes:** Use better light (morning window), clean outfit, no clutter in background. Slow-mo on the best transitions. Light instrumental music.

**Hook:**  
*"5 minutes. This is all you need to start your day right."*

---

### REEL 11 — Friday
**Title:** The One Drill I Do Every Single Day  
**Pillar:** Longevity  
**Duration:** 30–40s  
**Vibe:** Personal, direct to camera, honest

**What to film:**  
Show the dead hang. Yes, just hanging from a bar.
1. Show you grabbing the bar and hanging (passive)
2. Then active — shoulders packed, engaged
3. Slow shoulder circles while hanging
4. Side to side weight shift

**Hook:**  
Direct to camera first: *"I don't skip this. Not one day in the last 6 months."* Then show it.

**What to say:**
- "Decompresses your spine"
- "Builds grip and shoulder stability"
- "Takes 2 minutes"
- "You can hang from a door frame if you don't have a bar"

**Caption hook:**  
*"The cheapest investment in your spine's health. A bar and 2 minutes."*

### REEL 12 — (Midweek / Lead Magnet Tease)
**Title:** My 3-Phase Training Week Explained  
**Pillar:** BTS  
**Duration:** 45–60s  
**Vibe:** Talking head + whiteboard or text overlay breakdown

**What to film:**  
You talking directly to camera. Break down your week in 3 phases:

## General Recording Tips

### Camera Setup
- Phone on a tripod or stack of books/yoga blocks
- Film in **4K 30fps** or **1080p 60fps** (for slow-mo option)
- **Portrait (9:16)** for Reels — always
- Natural light: morning window light is free and beautiful

### What to Wear
- Same style every week builds brand recognition
- Solid colors work best — avoid busy patterns
- Fitted but not tight — people need to see your joints and alignment

### Audio
- Ambient sound is underrated — mat sounds, breath, environment
- For voice-overs: record voice separately in a quiet room, then sync in CapCut
- Lo-fi / instrumental music: use CapCut's free library or Epidemic Sound

### Batching (record everything on the weekend)
- **Saturday or Sunday:** film all 3 reels back to back in one session
- Change outfit between reels if you want variety — same location is fine
- After the reels, spend 30min on pure B-roll: close-ups of hands, feet, KB, mat
- Edit everything Sunday so the week is fully loaded and scheduled before Monday

### Editing in CapCut
- Trim dead air at start and end (first frame = you already in motion)
- Add text overlays for key cues — white text, clean font
- Slow-mo: 50% speed on the best movement moments
- Keep total edit time under 2h per reel when starting out
