```

Wrong: rushing it, shrugging, arching the low back

Do it right and you're building shoulder health that lasts decades.

3 sets of 5 each direction. That's all.

### Reel 5 — Kettlebell Armbar

```
One move. Shoulder stability. Thoracic mobility. That's the deal.

The kettlebell armbar is the most underrated drill I know.

Nobody talks about it. Everyone who does it feels the difference within a week.

What it trains:
→ Rotator cuff strength (active, not passive)
→ Thoracic rotation
→ Hip extension and core stability
→ Keeps your eye on the bell the whole time = focus + proprioception

This is yoga and kettlebell work in a single movement.

How to do it:
→ Lie on your back, KB pressed straight up
→ Slowly roll to the side, keeping your arm vertical
→ Let your hips open — find extension at the bottom
→ Return slowly. Control is everything.

3 reps each side. Move like you mean it.

### Reel 6 — 3-Minute Wrist Prep

```
Wrists always hurt during training? You're skipping this.

Before any push-up, handstand, or downward dog — your wrists need to be ready.

Most wrist pain during training is a prep problem, not a strength problem.

The 5-step wrist warm-up:
→ Wrist circles — 10 each direction
→ Fingers forward, lean in — 30s hold
→ Fingers back, lean in — 30s hold
→ Palm-down press hold — 20s
→ Prayer hands, elbows out — 20s

3 minutes. Do it every time.

After a few weeks of this consistently — you'll forget wrist pain was ever a thing.

Save this for your next session. 🙏

### Reel 9 — Cossack Squat Progressions

```
One move. Inner thighs. Hips. Ankle mobility. Glute strength. You're welcome.

The Cossack squat might be the most complete lower body mobility drill you can do.

Most people skip it because it looks hard.
But there are 3 levels — and everyone can start somewhere.

Level 1 — Beginner:
→ Hands on floor for balance, partial range of motion
→ Focus: just getting comfortable with the lateral weight shift

Level 2 — Intermediate:
→ Hands at chest, full depth
→ Focus: keep the heel of the bent leg flat on the floor

Level 3 — Advanced:
→ Slow tempo with a reach or thoracic rotation at the bottom
→ Focus: control, not speed

Start where you are. Stay there until it's easy. Then go to the next level.

Which level are you at right now? Drop it in the comments 👇
