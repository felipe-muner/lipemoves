# Filming System — Fast, Scalable, Repeatable
### How to go from 0 to fully posted in one weekend session

---

## The Core Principle

Treat filming like a production line, not a creative session.
Creative decisions happen BEFORE you press record — not during.
On filming day: you already know every movement, every angle, every outfit. You just execute.

## The 90-Minute Sunday Session

This is your weekly production sprint. Non-negotiable. Block it in your calendar.

### Phase 1 — Warm up (10 min)
- Don't film cold
- Light movement: hip circles, shoulder rolls, cat-cow, wrist prep
- Get your body into the movements you're about to film

### Phase 2 — Film Reel 1 (20 min)
- Set up angle, press record, do 3 takes
- Pick the best take on the spot (don't review all footage — trust your gut)
- Move on immediately

### Phase 3 — Film Reel 2 (20 min)
- Change outfit if needed (2 min max — have it ready)
- Same process: 3 takes, pick the best, move on

### Phase 4 — Film Reel 3 (15 min)
- Same

### Phase 5 — B-roll pass (15 min)
- This is gold — do it every single week
- Close-up of hands on KB
- Feet on mat
- Equipment flat lay
- You walking into frame
- Slow pan across your setup
- These clips get reused across weeks — you build a library

### Phase 6 — TikTok raw clip (10 min)
- Phone in hand, face camera, talk for 60–90 seconds
- No script — just riff on something you noticed during filming
- Raw and real — this is TikTok energy, not Instagram energy

**Total: 90 minutes. 3 edited reels worth of footage + a growing B-roll library.**

## The Scalable Filming Rules

### Rule 1 — Always film 3 takes per reel
Never film just one. Never film more than three. Three is the number.
- Take 1: warm-up take — you'll probably not use it but it loosens you up
- Take 2: usually the best — you're warm, not overthinking
- Take 3: backup — in case take 2 had a technical issue

### Rule 2 — Never review footage during filming
Film all three takes. Don't stop to watch them. Keep momentum.
Review after the full session is done.

### Rule 3 — Same location until 1k followers
Don't waste time finding new spots. One clean background, consistent light.
Familiarity builds brand identity faster than variety.
When you hit 1k — then explore new locations.

### Rule 4 — Same outfit every week, no exceptions
One outfit. Every reel. Every week. This is your brand signature.
People will start to recognize you by it — like a uniform.
Lay it out the night before so there's zero decision fatigue on filming day.

### Rule 5 — Film portrait (9:16) always
Every reel, every take. Don't film landscape and crop — you lose quality and framing.

### Rule 6 — Slow-mo for everything
Film everything in 1080p 60fps. In CapCut you can choose to use it at normal speed OR slow it down to 50%. This gives you options in the edit without reshooting.

## The Edit System (Sunday, after filming — 60 min)

### CapCut Workflow (per reel — 15–20 min each)

1. **Import** the best take
2. **Trim** — cut dead air at start and end (first frame = already in motion)
3. **Speed** — slow down key moments to 50% (transitions, deep positions)
4. **Text overlays** — white text, clean font, 2–4 cues max per reel
5. **Music** — pick from CapCut library (search "lo-fi", "calm", "minimal") OR no music (ambient sound)
6. **Hook text** — first 2 seconds, large text overlay with your hook line
7. **Export** — 1080p, save to camera roll

**Do not over-edit.** Simple is better. Clean cuts, a few text overlays, done.

### Canva Workflow (carousels — 20 min for 2 carousels)

1. Use one template — same every week (build it once, duplicate it)
2. Swap text and images only
3. Slide 1 = hook (big bold text)
4. Slides 2–5 = value (one point per slide)
5. Last slide = CTA + your handle

## The B-Roll Library System (scales over time)

Every Sunday you add ~15 new clips to your library.
By month 2 you have 60+ clips.
By month 3 you have 150+ clips.

This library means:
- You can make a carousel reel without filming anything new
- TikTok posts take 10 minutes (talk over existing B-roll)
- YouTube Shorts = your best reel + 30 extra seconds of B-roll

Organise in a folder on your phone:
```
LipeMoves/
  b-roll/
    hands/
    floor-movements/
    kettlebell/
    wide-shots/
  reels/
    week-01/
    week-02/
  raw/
```

## The Daily Habit (5 min — not negotiable)

Every single day during the week — even when you don't post:

**Stories (3 slides, 5 min):**
1. Morning: a thought or question ("What's your morning routine?")
2. Training: a raw clip from your session — no edit, just raw
3. Evening: tip of the day or a question for followers

This is your relationship engine. The feed posts build reach. Stories build trust.

## Month 1 → Month 3 Scaling Path

### Month 1 — Learn the system
- 3 reels/week, 2 carousels, daily stories
- Focus: get comfortable on camera, learn CapCut, find your angle
- Time: 8–10h/week

### Month 2 — Add TikTok
- Same Sunday session + 10 min raw TikTok clip
- Repurpose your best IG reel to TikTok and YouTube Shorts
- Time: 9–11h/week (barely more)

### Month 3 — Add YouTube Shorts
- Best reel of the week goes to YouTube Shorts with a longer intro
- Start thinking about your first long-form YouTube video (15 min tutorial)
- Time: 10–12h/week

### Month 4+ — Repurpose everything
By this point you have a library. New content can be:
- A clip from a 6-week-old reel with a new caption
- A compilation of your B-roll with a voiceover
- A "revisiting" post where you update an old drill

One hour of filming in month 1 = 3 months of repurposable content.
